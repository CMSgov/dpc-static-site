Fill gaps in patients' histories for timely, coordinated care

Data at the Point of Care (DPC) gives providers access to events in their patients’ histories that may be unavailable through current records. An application programming interface (API), DPC captures data from Medicare claims and shares it with providers. DPC data is standardized to meet Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) requirements and integrates easily with different systems.

Unlock the value of Medicare claims

Access data including:

  • New diagnoses
  • Medications
  • Completed procedures
  • Preventative care
  • External encounters

Easily integrate data with health records

DPC shares Medicare A, B, and D claims data in HL7 Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources (FHIR) format. FHIR complies with Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) and integrates with existing health records.

Get the whole story of each patient’s journey

Medicare claims data bridges gaps across provider networks. Get information about visits, diagnoses, treatments, and medications for smoother transitions, less paperwork, and more coordinated care.

Remove patient burden and personalize care

DPC makes sure Medicare beneficiaries don’t have to remember all the details in their history of care. These patients appreciate when providers know who they are and can spend more time listening and interacting with them.

DPC data in action

Wellness checks - Maria

Maria, 68 years old, knows she got the first dose of her shingles vaccine at her local pharmacy, but can't remember when. With DPC data, her doctor sees the vaccination date along with her other records and can tell her if she needs her next dose.

Population analytics - Elena

Elena, a diabetes care coordinator, manages the care of dozens of patients with chronic conditions. She uses DPC data to stay informed and perform analytics on recent clinic visits, hospital admissions, procedures, and medication refills for her entire population of patients.
