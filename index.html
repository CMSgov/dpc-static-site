A 360° view of your patients'
claim history

As patients move throughout the healthcare system, providers often struggle to gain and maintain a complete picture of their patients’ medical history. Data at the Point of Care (DPC) aims to fill these gaps in care by providing Medicare Fee-For-Service claims data to providers in a structured and standardized format.

Use cases for DPC

{% include sprite.html icon="insights" size="8" %}

Get more data

Learn about new diagnoses, medications, completed procedures, and preventive items on your patients' visits to other clinics, including urgent care, hospitals, and ER visits.

Manage less paperwork

Facilitate seamless transitions of care across provider teams by sharing a fuller picture of the patient's care.

Improve quality of care

Spend less time tracking down patient records or relying on the patient's memory for their medical history. Spend more time delivering better care and addressing patient needs during the visit.

Wellness checks

Maria, a 68-year-old Medicare beneficiary, recalls getting the first dose of her shingles vaccine at her local pharmacy earlier this year, but she can't remember when she went. Her provider uses data from DPC to see when Maria got her shot, and if she's ready for her second dose.

{% include sprite.html icon="identification" size="7" %}

Population analytics

Elena, a diabetes care coordinator, manages the care of dozens of patients with chronic conditions. She uses DPC data to stay informed and perform analytics on recent clinic visits, hospital admissions, procedures, and medication refills for her entire population of patients.

How can I get my healthcare organization involved?

In order to receive claims data for Medicare beneficiaries, the DPC API requires a partnership between providers and their Health IT team to develop and design a solution that seamlessly integrates with the Health IT software. This capability empowers providers to request claims data directly through the Health IT software and gain insight into patient records within the existing workflows utilized at the point of care.
