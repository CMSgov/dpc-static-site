Get the full story on every patient

Data at the Point of Care (DPC) fills gaps in your patient's health histories to support high quality care. Medicare claims contain valuable patient data. Centers for Medicare & Medicaid Services (CMS) shares this data across providers for personalized, coordinated care.

DPC data in action

Personalized care

Thanks to DPC data, Sarah, a primary care doctor, spends less time asking questions and more time listening to her patients. She sees more of what she needs to know about every beneficiary in the same health record system she uses every day.

Patient wellness checks

Maria, 68 years old, knows she got the first dose of her shingles vaccine at her local pharmacy, but can’t remember when. With DPC data, her doctor sees the vaccination date along with her other records and can tell her if she needs her next dose.

Provider analytics

Elena, a diabetes care coordinator, manages the care of dozens of patients with chronic conditions. She uses DPC data to stay informed and perform analytics on recent clinic visits, hospital admissions, procedures, medication refills for her entire patient population.

Integrate with health records systems

DPC shares Medicare A, B, and D claims data in standardized, HL7 Fast Healthcare Interoperability Resources (FHIR) format. FHIR integrates easily with provider health records.

{% include sprite.html icon="medical_services" size=6 %}

Coordinate across care teams

Medicare claims data bridges gaps across provider networks. This means less paperwork and smoother transitions between providers.

{% include sprite.html icon="identification" size=6 %}

Remove patient burden

When patients no longer need to relate their health histories, providers can spend more time on treatment, education, and answering questions. Patients feel seen and understood.
